UK Panel Show Gender Breakdown

Key:  male  /  female 

By @astronomyblog. Stats last updated 1 October 2015

Generally, panel shows on UK television include few female guests. Most have male hosts and male captains/regulars. Even omitting the regulars, the guests are mostly men too. It is a problem that half the population are under-represented in these long-running TV and radio shows.

Is the situation really that bad? In short, yes. I've compiled some statistics for current, long-running, shows from comedy.co.uk, Love Graphs, just-a-minute.info, IMdB and Wikipedia to illustrate the point. The table below is split into comedy panel shows and others. Many of them are comedy panel shows so I've made a non-exhaustive list of funny women to provide inspiration for the show makers.

Discussion

Nearly all these long-running shows under-represent women even if you ignore the regulars. The only show to have equal representation amongst guests is Listomania! Obviously, the rest still have a long way to go.

IMDb seems to be missing a fraction of guest appearances for some of these shows and lists more than implied by the panel size for others (presumably due to special guests). The real situation may be better (or worse) than it looks.

This analysis was done automatically and relies on IMDB pages to be complete/up-to-date. There may very well be mistakes made during the parsing of the data. These data are not perfect but they are broadly representative. If the show makers provide more accurate/complete stats I'll be happy to include those on this page.
